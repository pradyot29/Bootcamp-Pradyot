Design Review

Review Criteria

When reviewing my peers' designs, I focus primarily on how well the website performs and how easily users can navigate through it. Here are the key areas I consider when evaluating a design:

  • User-centric design I look at how simple it is for users to navigate and achieve their goals. This includes the site’s layout and overall user experience
  • Content Clarity I focus on how the content is presented. Is it easy to digest?
  • Visual Harmony Make sure the design elements align with each other and create a cohesive whole. The choice of images, typography, and spacing should work together for aesthetics.
  • Technical Efficiency Pay close attention to how quickly pages load and how well the site runs.
  • Responsiveness: I make sure the website works well on various devices and screen size

Currently, as I’m building my UI/UX expertise through this bootcamp, my perspective leans more towards how well the design works and serves its users, rather than focusing on the visual appeal..
